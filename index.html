Third Philippine Small-Scale Fisheries National Symposium

October 21-23, 2026 | Batangas State University, Batangas City, Luzon, Philippines

Symposium Countdown

Framework Alignment Overview

The first two Philippines Small-Scale Fisheries National Symposia established a clear national consensus: that small-scale fisheries (SSF) are too vital to ignore, and that the country must move from recognition to sustained action. Across many coastal communities, community-based and co-management fisheries initiatives have already demonstrated measurable gains in resource recovery, local governance, gender inclusion, and livelihood diversification.

However, these successful models often remain localized, fragmented, and insufficiently documented, limiting their potential for wider replication and policy integration. Anchored on the theme “From Action to Replication: Scaling Up and Deepening the Impact of Small-Scale Fisheries,” the Third Philippines Small-Scale Fisheries National Symposium seeks to advance the discourse from isolated success stories toward systematic scaling, adaptation, and institutionalization of proven practices.

Global & Strategic Alignment

The symposium is guided by a central question: How can effective small-scale fisheries initiatives be replicated and scaled to generate sustainable, inclusive, and long-term impact across Philippine coastal communities? This initiative aligns seamlessly with regional, domestic, and global baseline paradigms:

  • Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO) Voluntary Guidelines for Securing Sustainable Small-Scale Fisheries
  • TBTI’s Global Small-Scale Fisheries Research Agenda
  • Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) 1, 2, 5, 10, 13, 14, and 16
  • Philippine Blue Economy and Climate Adaptation frameworks

Announcement

About Us & Legacy Tracking

The NATIONAL CONSORTIUM FOR SMALL SCALE FISHERIES RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT OR TOO-BIG-TO-IGNORE (TBTI) PHILIPPINES is a research network and knowledge mobilization partnership that focuses on addressing issues and concerns affecting the viability and sustainability of small-scale fisheries (SSF) in the Philippines. The symposium organizers highly encourage and welcome submissions focusing on the diverse aspects of small-scale fisheries. Research, case studies, and field experiences addressing governance, environmental resilience, socioeconomic dynamics, cultural heritage, and technological innovations within small-scale fisheries are eligible for presentation. The symposium serves as an inclusive platform for researchers, academics, practitioners, civil society organizations, and students to share insights, engage in meaningful dialogue, and contribute to the sustainable development of small-scale fisheries in the Philippines and the broader region.

Our Vision

TBTI Philippines envisions being the lead organization for policy inputs and advice on SSF matters in the country.

Our Mission

Its mission is to promote the conduct of transdisciplinary SSF research and governance to promote the welfare of small-scale fishers.

Our Goals

  • Goal 1: Conduct transdisciplinary research on SSF in the country.
  • Goal 2: Promote transdisciplinary SSF research and governance in the country.
  • Goal 3: Conduct training and other capability-building activities in line with the promotion of SSF transdisciplinary research.
  • Goal 4: Create a repository of SSF data, research, and other information in the country.
  • Goal 5: Provide inputs to policies affecting SSF in the country.
  • Goal 6: Promote the full implementation of Voluntary Guidelines for Securing Sustainable Small-Scale Fisheries (SSF Guidelines) in the country.

Rotational Symposia Legacy

The Philippine Small-Scale Fisheries National Symposium is organized through a rotational hosting arrangement among forty-eight (48) state colleges and universities offering Fisheries programs. This approach promotes national collaboration and encourages wider regional participation in fisheries research, education, and policy dialogue.

The First Philippine Small-Scale Fisheries National Symposium held last October 16-18, 2024 was hosted by the University of the Philippines Visayas, a leading institution in marine and fisheries research in the Philippines. The Second Philippine Small-Scale Fisheries National Symposium was subsequently hosted by Mindanao State University in Marawi City, Mindanao, expanding the symposium’s reach and engagement with stakeholders from the southern Philippines.

Continuing this collaborative tradition, the Third Philippine Small-Scale Fisheries National Symposium (PSSFNS 3) will be hosted by Batangas State University in Batangas City, Luzon on October 21-23, 2026, further strengthening nationwide participation and fostering continued dialogue among fisheries stakeholders and academic institutions.

Main Organizer

Batangas State University (BatStateU), officially designated as the Philippines’ National Engineering University by virtue of Republic Act No. 11694, stands as the country’s largest engineering institution with over 64,000 students across 12 campuses in CALABARZON. Boasting a 120-year legacy, this Level IV, ISO 9001:2015 certified state university is globally recognized for its academic excellence, holding prestigious ABET accreditations for its engineering, computer science, and information technology programs, alongside multiple CHED Centers of Excellence and Development. Beyond engineering, BatStateU serves as a vital economic and innovation hub, hosting the country's first PEZA-registered Knowledge, Innovation, and Science Technology (KIST) Park and various regional research centers. Strongly committed to the 2030 Global Goals, the university actively integrates environmental stewardship through its Center for Sustainable Development—earning it a spot as the 239th World's Most Sustainable University in the 2025 UI GreenMetric rankings—while simultaneously securing high marks in the 2025 WURI and THE Impact Rankings as a premier institution developing tomorrow's leaders for the global knowledge economy.

Abstract Submission

Abstract Submission Rules

The core scientific abstract guidelines apply directly to all technical submissions intended for General Sessions (Scientific Oral and Poster) as well as Student Competitions. Only strictly scientific, research-driven text parameters are permitted.

Scientific Guidelines & Font Layout Requirements

  • Presentation Modes: Authors must specify their preference for either Oral Presentation or Poster Presentation during the portal submission process.
  • Content Standards: Material must present factual, empirical research, case studies, or structured field experiences addressing small-scale fisheries themes. Creative interpretations, narrative writing formats, or unstructured artistic accounts are completely excluded.
  • Title Format: Maximum of 25 words, written in BOLD CAPITAL LETTERS, using 14-point Arial font. Taxonomic and scientific names must be italicized where applicable.
  • Author Listing: Co-authors written in 12-point Arial font; institutional affiliations written in 10-point Arial font. The name of the presenting author must be in bold letters, supplemented with the email address of the corresponding and/or presenting author.
  • Body Specifications: The content abstract text must be a single paragraph of not more than 300 words, composed in 12-point Arial font with 1.15 line spacing. Paragraphs must be separated by a blank line and must not contain indents.
  • Keywords: Precisely 3 to 5 keywords must be included at the bottom of the structure.
  • Official Language: All entries must be structured and presented strictly in English.
TEMPLATE PREVIEW STYLE:
AN EMPIRICAL ANALYSIS OF TRADITIONAL FISHING NET EFFICIENCY IN BATANGAS BAY
Juan A. Dela Cruz, Maria B. Santos
Department of Fisheries, Batangas State University, Batangas City, Philippines
delacruz.j@batstateu.edu.ph

This study investigates the structural performance and yield rates of selective gillnets used within small-scale coastal municipal waters... (Max 300 words, 1.15 spacing, Arial 12)

Keywords: Gillnets, Artisanal Fisheries, Catch Efficiency, Batangas Bay

Student Competitions

Competition Track Management

PSSFNS 3 provides a competitive platform for graduate and undergraduate student researchers. In line with the updated symposium directives, all categories enforce standard scientific structures. Artistic or dramatic modifications are strictly prohibited.

Student Oral Speed Presentation Competition

Open to both undergraduate and graduate student levels. Submissions must adhere entirely to the prescribed scientific abstract standards.

  • Presentation Rule: Presentations must follow a structured scientific layout. Creative or artistic delivery parameters—such as delivering summaries via songs, poems, dramatic setups, or theatrical presentations—are completely prohibited.
  • Media Formatting Rules: Slides must look highly professional. Fancy transitions, slide animations, background audio loops, artistic video breaks, and embedded GIFs of any style are banned to protect the research focus.
  • Deliverables: Accepted entries must upload a 5-minute prerecorded video displaying the speaker next to their slides, together with a written presentation script, on or before October 4, 2026.
  • Slide Controls: Presenters may use either 1 static slide or a maximum limit of 5 slides. Each slide must include page tracks positioned on the bottom right corner in the format: Page x of y.
  • Language: Delivery must be completed entirely in English.

Student Poster Presentation Competition

Open to graduate and undergraduate students whose abstract summaries are officially accepted by the Review Committee.

  • Poster Structure: Deliverables must follow a rigid scientific outline containing the following sections: Title; Authors and Affiliations; Introduction (with Background and Objectives); Materials and Methods; Results (displayed via structured tables, graphs, or charts); Conclusions and Recommendations; References; and Acknowledgments.

"Fishing for Solutions" Competition

A specialized challenge targeted toward student teams proposing functional, empirical, or logistical solutions to immediate gaps within local fisheries.

  • Eligibility: Open to undergraduate and graduate groups. Teams must consist of 3 to 5 student members and 1 designated faculty mentor.
  • Abstract Requirements: Abstract outlines must describe an innovative solution addressing an active small-scale fishing issue, utilizing the official abstract template. Submissions must be uploaded via the portal on or before July 31, 2026. Language must be strictly English.
